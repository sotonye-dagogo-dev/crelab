Creator Spotlight: Adunola Okonkwo, Content Creator

Crelab Team · Jul 2026 · 5 min read
Creator Spotlights Lagos

Behind every great piece of brand content is a creator who understood the brief better than the client expected. We sat down with Adunola Okonkwo, a Lagos-based content creator specialising in beauty and lifestyle UGC, to understand how she built her career without waiting for her follower count to make the decisions for her.

The Brief is Everything

Adunola's philosophy is simple: brands don't hire you for your audience. They hire you for your ability to tell their story. She lists the 3-step brief framework she uses with every brand — and why she never starts a shoot without a written scope.

"The brief is your contract. If it's vague, the feedback will be, too."

She explains that a good brief should define the audience first, not the product. Most brands send her a brief that describes what they want to say before they've decided who they want to say it to. Once the audience is clear, the format and tone follow naturally. Then and only then does she talk about deliverables and deadlines.

Pricing Without Apology

When asked about rates, Adunola is direct. She shares how she determined her package pricing and why transparent packages on Crelab have increased her booking quality over volume. She used to quote bespoke rates for every inquiry, but found that clients who hesitated on price also hesitated on creative trust. Fixed packages with clear deliverables attracted brands who understood the value of professional content from the start.

Her basic UGC package starts at ₦45,000 for a single 30–60 second video with basic colour grading and one revision round. The standard package at ₦85,000 includes three videos, full editing, captions, and two revision rounds — delivered within five days. Premium clients pay ₦150,000 for five videos, same-day edits, and priority scheduling.

Why Crelab?

Adunola joined Crelab because she was tired of chasing payments and negotiating scope on social media DMs. She wanted a platform where her portfolio did the talking and where escrow protected both her time and the client's budget. Since joining, she says the quality of inbound inquiries has been consistently higher — brands arrive already informed about her rates and style.

Advice for Creators

Her advice to new creators is characteristically blunt: "Your portfolio is your CV. Stop worrying about your follower count and start worrying about whether your first three videos make someone want to hire you." She recommends every creator invest in one strong piece of spec work for their ideal client, rather than spreading themselves thin across content formats they don't enjoy.
